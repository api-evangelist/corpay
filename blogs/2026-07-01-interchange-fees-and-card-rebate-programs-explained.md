An interchange fee is the per-transaction charge a supplier's bank pays the buyer's card-issuing bank when a card is used, with the card network setting the rate. For businesses paying suppliers by commercial or virtual card, interchange fees fund the rebate earned on that spend, making them a revenue source rather than a cost.
